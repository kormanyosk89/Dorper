Behind the Flock

I have been involved in sheep breeding for more than a decade, and I first encountered the Dorper breed during my university years under the professional guidance of Dr. János Oláh. It quickly became clear to me that this breed is not only unique but also holds serious long-term potential.

Since then, I have been building the flock through conscious and consistent breeding work. Today, the herd consists of around 100 animals and is located in Békés County, Hungary, near Kaszaper.

What I recognized in the Dorper breed is exactly what is becoming increasingly important under European conditions: adaptability, resilience, and cost-efficiency. The breed performs well even under more extreme weather conditions, utilizes feed efficiently, and produces excellent meat conformation.

My goal is not quantity, but to establish a reliable, uniform genetic foundation that provides long-term value. In addition to selling breeding animals, I also support new farmers with professional advice. Delivery can be arranged upon request.
